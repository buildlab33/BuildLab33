## BRAND & BUSINESS

Q1. What are Yeon Studios and BeLive Studios — same business, separate brands, or parent/sub-brand?
> Complementary brands within a unified ecosystem. BeLive Studios is the content and IP engine — focused on storytelling, microdrama creation, and production. Yeon Studios is the infrastructure layer. Together they form a vertically integrated content and distribution ecosystem, from IP creation to audience delivery.

Q2. What industry do we operate in? (live streaming, photography, events, media-tech, or mix?)
> Media-tech, OTT infrastructure, and digital content/IP production — with a strong focus on short-form and microdrama formats.

Q4. What is our key differentiator for BeLive Studios?
> A full-stack IP studio with strong capabilities in microdrama development, cinematic storytelling, and agile production, built for high-frequency content output. Differentiation lies in owning both the content/IP layer and the distribution platform, allowing tighter control over monetisation and audience data.

## CONTENT & SOCIAL MEDIA

Q5. Which platforms need managing for BeLive Studios?
> Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook. X only if there is an active publishing strategy.

Q6. What type of content should we post?
> Thought leadership, behind-the-scenes, case studies, client wins, portfolio highlights, event recaps, promotional posts.

Q7. How many posts per platform per week?
> Instagram: 3–5 | LinkedIn: 2–4 | TikTok: 3–5 | YouTube: 1–2 (or biweekly) | Facebook: 2–3 | X: 3–7 only if actively maintained.

Q8. Do we have an existing content library the agent can repurpose?
> Yes — past photos, videos, testimonials, event footage, case studies, press mentions, internal brand assets.

Q9. What is our primary goal — rank by priority.
> 1. Generate high-quality inbound leads and strategic partnerships. 2. Position as category leader in microdrama production and IP ecosystems. 3. Build IP awareness. 4. Attract creative talent. 5. Establish industry authority.

Q10. Any competitors or brands we want to benchmark against?
> Three benchmark layers: Content/IP (top-tier studios and microdrama leaders), Infrastructure/OTT (platforms enabling direct-to-consumer content ecosystems), Creative/Brand (studios and agencies with strong narrative and cultural impact). Intent: define a new vertically integrated category, not replicate.

## BRAND VOICE

Q12. Mobile app preference — web-based or native iOS/Android?
> Web-based first — faster, lower cost, easier to iterate. Native mobile can come later if needed.

Q13. Budget range and target timeline?
> Define MVP budget first, then phase in automation, analytics, outreach, and visual generation after launch.

Q14. What has been tried before — and what didn't work?
> To be documented internally to avoid repeating weak tactics.

Q15. Who will review and approve content before it posts?
> Human approval first. Review-before-post for branded/client-facing content. Autonomous posting only for low-risk pre-approved templates. CEO handles strategic approvals only.

Q17. Any topics, tones, or angles strictly off-limits?
> Political content, sensitive claims, unverified client results, competitor attacks, off-brand tone.

Q18. How should BeLive Studios sound?
> Cinematic, emotionally driven, and culturally sharp. Focused on storytelling, IP creation, and audience connection. Overall tone: confident, future-facing, and category-defining.

Q19. Do we have existing brand guidelines or past content?
> Foundational elements exist. Will formalize: unified narrative across infrastructure and IP layers, cinematic premium visual language, content system tailored for OTT-first and short-form storytelling.

## OUTREACH

Q20. Who are the key people we want to reach?
> Brand managers, marketing leads, agency heads, event organizers, media partners, journalists, potential collaborators.

Q21. Should the agent draft outbound outreach emails and DMs?
> Yes — outreach messages, follow-ups, collaboration pitches, press emails.

Q22. What does success look like in 3 months?
> 3–5 strategic OTT or content partnerships, 10–20 qualified inbound leads, 2–3 press or industry features, strong positioning as category leader in microdrama production + IP creation, validated content-to-partnership pipeline.

## OPERATIONS & MARKET

Q23. What region/market are we targeting?
> Primary: Singapore. Secondary: Southeast Asia. Expansion: Korea, China, global via IP and distribution partnerships.

Q24. What language(s) should content be written in?
> English first. Bilingual only if target audience actively requires it.

Q25. Who will be operating this agent day-to-day?
> Kevin or a social media manager. CEO handles strategic approvals only.

Q26. Should the agent respond to inbound comments and DMs?
> Outbound content first. Controlled inbound response using approved reply templates can be added later.

Q27. Should the agent monitor industry news and suggest timely content?
> Yes — flag relevant trends, campaign angles, event moments, industry developments.

Q28. Do we want analytics and performance reporting?
> Yes — track performance by platform, post type, engagement, reach, clicks, lead indicators.

Q29. Should the agent track outreach?
> Yes — contact name, company, message sent, date, reply status, next follow-up, outcome.

Q30. Any tools we already use that this must integrate with?
> Email, Google Sheets, Notion, any CRM already in use.

## TECHNICAL

Q28A. Do we have developer/API access for social media accounts?
> If API access is limited, use a third-party scheduling tool first (faster, more reliable). Direct API integration can come later.

Q28B. Should the agent generate visual content or text captions only?
> Start with text captions, content ideas, simple branded graphics. AI-generated visuals later if they fit brand standards and approval rules.
